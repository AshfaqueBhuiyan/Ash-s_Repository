Bluebell: Hyacinthoides is classified in the subfamily Scilloideae (now part of the family Asparagaceae, but formerly treated as a separate family, called Hyacinthaceae), alongside genera such as Scilla and Ornithogalum.

Blue Lotus: Nymphaea caerulea, commonly called the blue lotus, blue water lily, or sacred blue lily, is a water lily native to the Nile region of Egypt and parts of East Africa. It is recognized by its striking blue petals and yellow center, floating gracefully on water surfaces. In ancient Egyptian culture, the blue lotus was associated with the sun, rebirth, and spiritual enlightenment.

Daisy: Leucanthemum vulgare, commonly known as the ox-eye daisy, oxeye daisy, dog daisy, marguerite, is a widespread flowering plant native to Europe and Asia, and an introduced plant to North America, Australia, and New Zealand.

Lily: Lilium candidum and other species of the genus Lilium, commonly known as lilies, are flowering plants native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. Lilies are well known for their large, often fragrant trumpet-shaped blooms and come in colors ranging from white and yellow to orange, pink, and red.

Sunflower: The common sunflower (Helianthus annuus) is a large annual forb of the genus Helianthus. It is commonly grown as a crop for its edible oily seeds.

Tulip: Tulips (Tulipa) are a genus of spring-blooming perennial herbaceous bulbiferous geophytes. The flowers are usually large, showy, and brightly coloured, generally red, pink, yellow, or white.
